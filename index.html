PARSHURAM JI
Parshuram, the sixth incarnation of Lord Vishnu, is a legendary figure in Hindu mythology known for his courage, devotion, and righteousness. Born to sage Jamadagni and Renuka, he belonged to the Brahmin class but had the qualities of a fierce warrior. His story begins with a vow to avenge the death of his father, who was unjustly killed by a Kshatriya king. In his rage, Parshuram is said to have wiped out the Kshatriya race 21 times to cleanse the earth of tyranny and injustice. Despite his anger, he remained deeply spiritual and was a devoted follower of dharma.

BAJI RAO PESHWA
Bajirao Peshwa I was one of the most celebrated military leaders in Indian history and served as the Prime Minister (Peshwa) of the Maratha Empire from 1720 to 1740. Known for his exceptional leadership and strategic brilliance, Bajirao expanded the Maratha influence far beyond Maharashtra, pushing into northern and central India. He is credited with over 40 major battles and numerous campaigns, never losing a single one, which earned him a legendary status.

DADA RAVAN
Ravan, the legendary king of Lanka from the Indian epic Ramayana, was a complex figure known for his immense intelligence, strength, and devotion. Despite being portrayed as the primary antagonist, Ravan possessed many admirable qualities. He was a brilliant scholar and a master of the Vedas, known for his deep knowledge of astrology, music, and statecraft. A powerful warrior and a formidable ruler, Ravan built a prosperous kingdom with advanced infrastructure. He was also a devout devotee of Lord Shiva, demonstrating intense spiritual dedication.
